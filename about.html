Our Story

Three generations of family, farming, and community.

Since 1962

Hollow Creek Farm has been part of the Calloway family since 1962. What began as a small vegetable operation has grown into one of the region's most beloved agritainment destinations. Today, visitors enjoy seasonal activities, fresh produce, family events, and a welcoming farm experience that celebrates the traditions of rural life.

Founders Bill and Margaret Calloway passed the farm to their daughter, Dana, and her husband, Rob, in 2008. Their children, Tyler, Meg, and Sam, grew up working in the fields, helping with harvests, and welcoming guests. Today they help manage the farm full-time, continuing the family's commitment to quality, community, and hospitality.

The 140-acre farm features working fields, a restored 1890s barn, sunflower fields, a pumpkin patch, a corn maze, a small orchard, and a covered pavilion for special events. Visitors often describe Hollow Creek Farm as the kind of place that makes you slow down and enjoy the simple moments.
